Entrepreneurship is commonly thought of as the process of starting a business. While this is true, in part, some nuances should be considered. By that definition, entrepreneurs—either as individuals or in teams—discover opportunities throughout their personal and professional lives. They form hypotheses about ways they can deliver value to customers and perform structured tests to validate their ideas. This often involves recruiting teammates and investing funds to determine how they’ll deliver a product or service at an acceptable cost.
